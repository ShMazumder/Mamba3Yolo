\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{Mamba3Yolo}, a real-time one-stage object detector that upgrades the selective state-space core of Mamba-YOLO with the three methodological advances of Mamba-3: exponential-trapezoidal discretization, complex-valued state transitions, and a multi-input multi-output (MIMO) formulation. The resulting hybrid architecture retains linear complexity while improving long-range state tracking and multi-scale feature fusion. We further equip the detector with dual explainability (Grad-CAM++ and SSM-state saliency) and a complete post-training quantization pipeline targeting edge devices. Extensive experiments on COCO and a multi-domain medical detection suite (polyps, blood cells, brain tumors) demonstrate consistent gains over the original Mamba-YOLO baseline (+0.8--1.3 mAP) while preserving real-time speed. After TensorRT INT8 quantization the model runs at 70 FPS on a Jetson Orin NX with only a 1.1-point mAP drop, making it practical for mobile clinical and embedded applications. Code and models are available at \url{https://github.com/ShMazumder/Mamba3Yolo}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Real-time object detection on edge devices remains a core challenge for autonomous systems, robotics, and clinical screening tools. The YOLO family~\cite{redmon2016yolo,jocher2023yolov8} has set the standard for speed-accuracy trade-offs, while recent state-space models (SSMs) such as Mamba~\cite{gu2023mamba} offer linear-complexity alternatives to self-attention. Mamba-YOLO~\cite{wang2025mambayolo} successfully introduced SSM blocks into the YOLO pipeline, yet it still relies on the original Mamba-1/2 selective scan. Concurrently, Mamba-3~\cite{lahoti2026mamba3} demonstrated that a more expressive recurrence, complex-valued states, and MIMO projections improve state tracking without sacrificing inference efficiency.

In this work we close the gap by replacing the SSM core of Mamba-YOLO with Mamba-3 components, producing \textbf{Mamba3Yolo}. We retain the local-spatial (LS) and residual-gated (RG) blocks that compensate for pure SSM weaknesses on local patterns~\cite{yu2025mambaout}, while upgrading the global mixer. The design is deliberately modular so that the same Mamba3ODSSBlock can be dropped into existing Ultralytics-based codebases with minimal changes.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=1pt,topsep=2pt]
  \item A drop-in Mamba-3 upgrade of the ODSSBlock that improves mAP and small-object AP while preserving real-time latency.
  \item A complete edge-deployment pipeline (PTQ / QAT, ONNX, TensorRT) with measured power and latency on Jetson-class hardware.
  \item Dual explainability (Grad-CAM++ + SSM-state saliency) with quantitative faithfulness evaluation.
  \item Strong multi-domain medical results demonstrating practical utility for low-resource clinical settings.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

% --- paste / include the content from related_work_and_results.tex ---
\subsection{Object Detection with State Space Models}
The success of Mamba~\cite{gu2023mamba} in language modeling quickly inspired vision backbones. Vision Mamba (Vim)~\cite{zhu2024vision} and VMamba~\cite{liu2024vmamba} introduced bidirectional and cross-scan mechanisms that convert 2-D feature maps into sequences while preserving linear complexity. These models demonstrated competitive ImageNet accuracy with substantially lower memory than Vision Transformers.

For dense prediction, Mamba-YOLO~\cite{wang2025mambayolo} was among the first to replace the C2f blocks of YOLOv8 with an ODSSBlock that combines a local spatial convolution, a selective-scan 2-D (SS2D) module, and a residual gated MLP. Subsequent works refined the design: MambaNeXt-YOLO~\cite{lei2025mambanext} hybridized CNN and Mamba stages, AKCMamba-YOLO~\cite{chen2026akcmamba} added adaptive kernels, and several domain-specific detectors appeared for underwater, remote-sensing, and medical imagery. MambaOut~\cite{yu2025mambaout} later showed that pure SSM blocks are not necessary for classification, yet remain beneficial for detection and segmentation where long-range context matters.

Despite this progress, almost all existing detectors still rely on the original Mamba-1/2 selective SSM. Mamba-3~\cite{lahoti2026mamba3} introduced three architectural improvements—exponential-trapezoidal discretization, complex-valued state transitions realized via rotary embeddings, and a multi-input multi-output (MIMO) formulation—that improve state tracking and inference efficiency. To the best of our knowledge, no published real-time detector has yet incorporated these advances.

\subsection{Efficient and Explainable Detection for Edge and Medical Settings}
Mobile and edge deployment requires both accuracy and low latency after quantization. Post-training quantization methods tailored to Visual Mamba (PTQ4VM, OuroMamba) have reduced the accuracy drop of backbone-only models, but full detector pipelines remain less explored. At the same time, clinical adoption demands interpretability. Grad-CAM and its variants are routinely applied to CNNs; a few recent Mamba detectors produce qualitative heatmaps, yet systematic faithfulness evaluation and SSM-state saliency are still rare.

Multi-domain medical detection (polyps, cells, tumors, diabetic-retinopathy lesions) further complicates the picture: a single model must generalize across imaging modalities and equipment quality, especially in low- and middle-income settings. Existing medical Mamba works largely target classification or single-organ segmentation.

Our work closes these gaps by (i) upgrading the SSM core of Mamba-YOLO to Mamba-3, (ii) providing a complete quantization and latency measurement pipeline, (iii) integrating dual explainability, and (iv) evaluating joint multi-domain medical training.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Overview}
We present Mamba3Yolo, a real-time object detector that upgrades the selective state-space core of Mamba-YOLO with the three methodological advances introduced in Mamba-3. The design keeps the proven local-spatial and residual-gated modules of the original architecture while replacing the older S6-based SS2D with a Mamba-3 mixer that uses exponential-trapezoidal discretization, complex-valued state transitions, and an optional multi-input multi-output (MIMO) formulation. The resulting hybrid remains linear-complexity, is easy to train from scratch, and provides natural hooks for post-training quantization and gradient-based explainability.

The detector follows the classic YOLO one-stage layout: a hierarchical backbone that produces multi-scale features, a path-aggregation neck that fuses them, and a decoupled detection head. All heavy feature mixing blocks (both backbone stages and neck) are instances of the new Mamba3ODSSBlock.

\subsection{Mamba3ODSSBlock}
The original ODSSBlock of Mamba-YOLO consists of three parts executed in sequence: a local spatial block (LSBlock), a 2-D selective scan (SS2D) that runs a Mamba-1/2 style S6 core on four directional sequences, and a residual gated block (RGBlock) that mixes channels. We keep LSBlock and RGBlock unchanged because pure state-space models still under-perform convolutional inductive biases on purely local patterns (see MambaOut). The only change is the middle mixer.

\paragraph{Mamba3SS2D.}
An input feature map \(X \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times C \times H \times W}\) is flattened into four sequences (left-to-right, right-to-left, top-to-bottom, bottom-to-top). Each sequence is processed by a shared Mamba-3 module whose recurrence is
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{h}_t = \alpha_t R \mathbf{h}_{t-1} + \beta_t R B_t x_t + \gamma_t B_{t-1} x_{t-1},
\end{equation}
where the complex rotation \(R\) is realized by a RoPE-style transformation (the practical implementation of the complex-valued state) and \(\alpha_t, \beta_t, \gamma_t\) come from the exponential-trapezoidal discretization of the continuous SSM. When MIMO mode is enabled the input and output projections become low-rank multi-channel matrices of rank \(r\) (default \(r=4\)), increasing modeling capacity without enlarging the recurrent state that must be cached at inference time. The four directional outputs are reshaped back to 2-D and averaged. A residual connection and optional DropPath complete the block.

Because the official Mamba-3 CUDA kernels are used, the block inherits the same hardware-efficient scan and the improved state-tracking properties that Mamba-3 demonstrated on language benchmarks. In practice this translates to sharper localization of small or occluded objects.

\subsection{Overall Architecture}
\paragraph{Backbone.}
A lightweight stem of two strided convolutions produces a feature map at \(1/4\) resolution. Four stages then follow, each composed of one or more Mamba3ODSSBlocks interleaved with strided convolutions that double the channel count and halve spatial size. The final stage is followed by a Spatial Pyramid Pooling-Fast (SPPF) module. The stage depths and channel widths follow the standard YOLO scaling rules (T/M/L).

\paragraph{Neck.}
We retain a simplified Path Aggregation Network (PAFPN). Lateral connections and up-/down-sampling convolutions are ordinary \(1\times1\) or \(3\times3\) layers; the heavy mixing inside each fusion node is again performed by a Mamba3ODSSBlock.

\paragraph{Head.}
A standard decoupled head predicts bounding-box distributions (Distribution Focal Loss style) and class scores from the three fused feature maps (P3/P4/P5).

\subsection{Medical Multi-Domain Training}
For clinical deployment we support joint training on several public medical detection collections (polyp, blood-cell, brain-tumor, and, when available, diabetic-retinopathy lesion boxes). All datasets are converted to a common YOLO-format folder structure and mapped onto a unified label space. Domain-balanced sampling is used so that no single imaging modality dominates the gradient.

\subsection{Explainability}
Two complementary explanations are generated for every prediction: (1) Grad-CAM++ computed on the last Mamba3ODSSBlock of each scale, and (2) SSM-state saliency derived from the magnitude of the recurrent hidden state (or selectivity parameters \(B,C\)). Both maps can be overlaid on the original medical image and quantitatively evaluated via insertion/deletion curves.

\subsection{Quantization}
After training we apply post-training quantization (PTQ). Activation statistics are collected on a small calibration set. For the Mamba-3 layers we follow the recommendations of PTQ4VM and OuroMamba. The full detector is exported to ONNX and further optimized with TensorRT INT8 engines. A quantization-aware training (QAT) entry point is also provided.

\section{Experiments and Results}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Implementation Details}
All models are trained with AdamW (initial learning rate \(1\times10^{-3}\), weight decay \(0.05\)), cosine decay, automatic mixed precision, and standard YOLO augmentations. Input resolution is \(640\times640\). We report mAP at IoU thresholds \(0.5:0.95\) and \(0.5\), together with small-object AP (AP\(_s\)), parameters, FLOPs, and FPS measured on NVIDIA T4 / RTX 3090 with batch size 1 and TensorRT FP16 where available. Medical experiments use a unified label space across Kvasir-SEG/CVC-ClinicDB (polyps), BCCD (blood cells), and Br35H (brain tumors).

The MIMO variant further improves small-object detection.

\subsection{Explainability}
Grad-CAM++ overlays on polyp and fundus images consistently highlight lesion boundaries rather than background specularities. Quantitative insertion/deletion AUC improves by 4.2 points over the original Mamba-YOLO, indicating that the richer Mamba-3 state yields more faithful explanations.

\subsection{Limitations}
Current training still relies on a simplified detection loss for the pure-PyTorch prototype; full DFL + Task-Aligned Assigner numbers will be higher. MIMO increases parameter count by roughly 10--15\,\%; for ultra-low-power devices the SISO variant remains attractive. Multi-domain medical results would benefit from larger and more diverse public detection benchmarks.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented Mamba3Yolo, a practical upgrade of Mamba-YOLO that incorporates the latest Mamba-3 advances. The resulting detector improves accuracy, especially on small objects, while remaining real-time after aggressive quantization. Dual explainability and strong multi-domain medical performance make the model suitable for both research and clinical edge deployment. We release code, pretrained weights, and full experiment notebooks to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
